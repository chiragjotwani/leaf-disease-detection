AI-Powered Plant Leaf Disease Detection

"Hypothesis: Automated plant disease detection using deep learning can enable early diagnosis, improve crop productivity, and support precision agriculture by providing accurate, real-time classification of plant health conditions from leaf images."

Problem Statement

Farmers often rely on manual visual inspection to detect plant diseases, which requires domain expertise and may lead to misdiagnosis. Delayed identification of infections results in crop loss, reduced yield, and increased pesticide usage. There is a need for an automated system capable of detecting diseases early with high accuracy and minimal human intervention.

Literature Review

Traditional approaches include manual inspection, rule-based image processing, and classical machine learning classifiers. These methods suffer from limited accuracy, dependence on handcrafted features, and poor generalization across crop types.

Research Gap / Innovation

The proposed system leverages transfer learning with MobileNetV2 to deliver lightweight, high-accuracy predictions in real time. It eliminates manual feature engineering and supports scalable deployment for agricultural applications.

System Methodology

Dataset / Input

PlantVillage dataset containing multiple plant species and disease classes. Images resized to 128×128, normalized, batched, and split into training and validation subsets.

Model Architecture

Image → MobileNetV2 Feature Extractor → Global Pooling → Dense Layers → Softmax Classifier for multi-class disease prediction.

Results & Analysis

Best Model MobileNetV2 (90.18%)

MobileNetV2 Accuracy: 90.18%

ResNet50 Accuracy: 28.87%

EfficientNetB0 Accuracy: 9.90%

MobileNetV2 achieved the highest validation accuracy among all tested architectures. With frozen pretrained layers and limited training epochs, the lightweight model generalized effectively on the PlantVillage dataset. ResNet50 and EfficientNetB0 require additional fine-tuning and extended training for improved performance.
